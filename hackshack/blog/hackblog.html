My projects

3D Scanner

The math behind turning 2D images into 3D pointclouds is facinating, and uses some of the concepts I've learned in math, like triganomitry and matricies. I've read several research papers on the subject, and most of the time the math goes right over my head. Receltly I stumbled apon a project on github written in python. By looking at the code I have been able to better understand the process, and hope to start writing my own code soon. A paper illistrating the concept well can be found here if you're interested. Soon we should be able to scan things!

Quadrotor

For christmas I got a new IMU! It's a board from sparkfun, combining an accelerometer and gyroscope into one. IMU's use sensors to understand the orientation of the sensor. Most phones have IMU's built into them! Using the workshop here I was able to gain a better understanding of how IMU's work, and wrote the code for one myself. Check it out if you're interested! A picture of the wiring for the IMU is below

Strandbeest

A couple weekends ago a couple friends and I went to see an exibat at a muesum featuring these creatures called "Strandbeests" Strandbeests are these crazy moving contraptions. The person who designs them considers them a species, because each year he redesigns the beest, fixing things that didn't work the year before, causing the creature to evolve. In the gift shop of the museum they were selling 3D printed strandbeests for $400, which is a little rediculus. I thought it would be cool to print one for the hack shack, so after a little research I found the BraBeest, a 3D-printable version of a strandbeest. I have printed some of the pieces, but the designed pegs are difficult to put together without breaking the plastic. After I find a way to put the pieces together I will print the rest of it, and the hack shack will have its very own Strandbeest! A picture of a full sized Strandbeest is below

Computer Vision

12-9-2015

It's been a while! The next step for the 3D scanner is detecting the lasers, and understanding the information they give me. After spending days, I got openCV installed and running. I was having building errors (because of a missing resourse), but ended up solving it using this guide. I spent time understanding how images work behind the scenes, and how they consist of enourmous lists of rgb values. After doing some more research on three demintanal arrays, I wrote a simple program to find the maximum red value in each line, and put a white dot there. Using this technique I can create a clear contour of the scanned image. A timelapse of a processed partial scan is below.

The next step is to analyze the created contours, and understand the distances the curves represent! I'm looking forward to the challenge. I also added a "preview" feature to the small images. It only uses css, and is a bit problimatic when it comes to multipul images, tell me what you think!

A Hack Shack Update

11-20-2015

Have you checked out the Hack Shack yet? Don't know what it is? Check out the video below! Learn from other students what kind of services we provide.

Over the past quarter, the Hack Shack has grown. After weeks of planning the Hack Shack opened up to the school on October 5th, providing students with opportunities and resources they couldn't find elsewhere. Since then we’ve been able to help people design and print 3D chickens, build annoying car alarms with LittleBits, and explore Italy using the Green Screen. The variety of opportunities offered by the Hack Shack are great, and will just keep expanding. The pictures below shows people designing things in Sketchup, as well as a study hall using the LittleBits.

The variety of projects and ideas the Hack Shack staff see daily continues to amaze us. If someone sets their mind to it, they can learn anything. We’re here to help you find the thing you want to learn about. No matter how intimidating coding or circuitry looks, you can learn how to do it. Seeing what people do in the Hack Shack could inspire you to follow a new career path that you wouldn't have even considered before. Sound interesting to you? Almost every period of the day, there are people in the Hack Shack who are happy to help you explore new ideas or help you with projects. Come on down!
