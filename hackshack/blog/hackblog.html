A Hack Shack Update

11-20-2015

Have you checked out the Hack Shack yet? Don't know what it is? Check out the video below! Learn from other students what kind of services this place provides.

Over the past quarter, the Hack Shack has grown. After weeks of planning the Hack Shack opened up to the school, providing students with opprotunities and resourses they couldn't find elsewhere. Since then I've been able to help people design and print chickens, build annoying car alarms, and explore Italy. The varity of opprotunities offered by the Hack Shack are great, and will just keep expanding. The pictures below show people designing things in Sketchup, as well as a study hall using the Little-Bits.

The varity of projects and ideas the Hack Shack staff see daily continues to amaze us. If someone sets their mind to it, they can learn anything. No matter how intimidating coding or circutry looks, you can learn how to do it. Seeing what people do in the Hack Shack could inspire you to follow a new career path that you woudn't have even considered before. Sound interesting to you? Almost every period of the day, there are people in the Hack Shack who are happy to help you explore new ideas or help you with projects. Come on down!

Test Runs and Software

11-9-2015

After hooking everything up and re-soldering the lasers, I started on the software. I just wrote a simple script that takes a picture with each laser turned on, then rotates the plate. There are problems with the stepper motor not moving at random intervals, and to fix this I will look deeper into how the motor works. Besides that I have to decide weather or not I want to use open sourse code for the scanner or learn to write it myself.

Last 3D Printed Parts

10-29-2015

Today I printed the last parts for the scanner. After the first camera mount didn't fit, I re-designed the mount. I added a slot in the bottom to fit a 20x20 profile. After this was printed (with new fillamint), and the nuts and bolts were bought, I discovered that the legs wouldn't hold the scanner steady. I designed simple vertical legs to attach to the 20x20's, and they did the trick. The pictures below is the re-designed camera mount.

Plan for the 3D Scanner

10-20-2015

The 3D scanner is starting to come together. Parts are starting to fit together, and as they do, I'm starting to realize that some pieces need to be reprinted. I want to print: a new motor attachment that has the plate attached to it already, a a camera mount with more room for the camera as well as a 20x20 mount, and lastly a properly lengthed 20x20 for the camera elevation. After this is done, it's time to move on to the software!
